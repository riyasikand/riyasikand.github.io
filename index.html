I am an undergraduate at Harvard University pursuing an A.B. in Computer Science with a double concentration in Molecular & Cellular Biology and a secondary in Government (Class of 2028). I am a John Harvard Scholar with a 4.0 GPA, passionate about applying computational methods and AI to solve problems at the intersection of biology, medicine, and technology.

My research spans cancer immunology, synthetic biology, and machine learning — from studying CD8+ T cell memory at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute to building biocomputing systems in bacteria at Harvard MCB Department's Cluzel Lab. I am especially interested in how AI and data-driven approaches can accelerate discovery in biomedicine.

Beyond research, I work at the intersection of technology, venture capital, and entrepreneurship — as a diligence analyst at Harvard Undergraduate Capital Partners, a former Strategy & Operations Lead at Wedge (YC S25), and a computational biology intern at Novaflow (YC S25). I also serve on the executive boards of Harvard Model Congress and the Harvard Undergraduate Biotechnology Club.

I also enjoy learning, and writing, about history — read my research here. I also competed as a finalist in the 2021 Regeneron ISEF.

Research

I work at the intersection of computational methods and experimental biology, with the goal of using AI and data-driven approaches to advance understanding of disease and accelerate therapeutic discovery. My current areas of focus include:

Cancer immunology & translational biology
🔬
Studying how cytokines and small molecule inhibitors modulate immune cell behavior, with a focus on CD8+ T cell memory potentiation in cancer contexts. Using wet lab techniques including flow cytometry, qPCR, and ELISA alongside computational analysis in R.
Machine learning for biomedical imaging & genomics
🧠
Developing and applying deep learning models — including CNNs, Vision Transformers, and self-supervised learning — to problems in medical imaging (brain MRI atrophy detection), environmental science (deforestation prediction), and infrastructure (pothole recognition).
Synthetic biology & biocomputing
🧬
Engineering genetic toggle switches in bacteria for digital biocomputing applications. Building and characterizing chromosomal and plasmid constructs using molecular cloning techniques, with potential applications in programmable therapeutics and biosensing.
Bioinformatics & multi-omics pipelines
⚙️
Building reproducible pipelines for genomics, transcriptomics, and multi-omics data processing at early-stage biotech startups. Working with cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP) to enable large-scale compute for production research deployments.
